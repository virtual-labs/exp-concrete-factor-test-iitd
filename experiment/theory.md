### Theory

- The compacting factor test of cement concrete determines its workability, especially performed for concrete with low workability. This test helps engineers and construction workers ensure that the concrete used in a construction project is of adequate consistency and when poured and compacted will perform the work well.

- **IS 1199 2018** talks about the procedure of performing the test and **IS 5515** talks about the apparatus specifications and its dimensions.

- The compaction factor is basically the ratio of weights of partially compacted concrete to that of fully compacted concrete.

- This test is used for concrete which has low workability, for which the concrete slump test is not suitable.

- Just like the previous test, based on the values obtained of compaction factor, the degree of workability can be assessed along with the location where that degree of workable concrete can be suitably employed.

- The primary purpose of this test is to determine how easily concrete can be placed and compacted, which is crucial for construction quality.
